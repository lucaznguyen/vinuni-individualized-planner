# VIP - 10-Minute Pitch Script

## 0:00-1:00 Hook and Problem Story

Good morning. My project is VIP, the VinUni Individualized Planner. The tagline is simple: see your load early, plan your week clearly, and ask the right person.

First-year students often do not struggle because of one assignment. The pressure appears when tasks from several courses quietly converge in the same week. A quiz, a lab, a writing reflection, and a group milestone may each look manageable alone. Together, they can become a heavy week before the student notices it.

The problem is not that VinUni lacks support. The problem is that students may not know early enough that they need help, or which support route fits their situation.

## 1:00-2:00 Key Insight

VinUni already has strong support foundations: office hours, PASS, advising, library support, the Health and Well-Being Center, and Peer Support Group.

VIP does not replace any of these services. Instead, it acts as a bridge between course deadlines and the support system students already have.

The key insight is that academic wellbeing should begin before crisis. If a student can see a heavy week earlier, they still have time to plan, ask one clarification question, attend office hour, or speak with an advisor.

## 2:00-3:00 VIP Solution

VIP turns syllabus-based deadlines into a student-owned planner. The first prototype is intentionally simple: no backend, no login, no external database, and no API calls.

The tool has four main outputs.

First, a Deadline Bank where students enter course tasks from the syllabus. Second, a weekly workload heatmap for Weeks 1 to 8. Third, a transparent Academic Load Score based on task size, deadline clustering, expected effort, and clarity risk. Fourth, an Action Card and Academic SOS Navigator that show what to do next and where to ask.

The score is not an AI model. It is a visible heuristic that helps a student understand why a week is Green, Yellow, or Red.

## 3:00-6:00 Live Demo Walkthrough

I will show three minutes of the prototype.

First, I open the Demo Cases tab and run Case 1: Hidden workload becomes visible. The dashboard loads a first-year student's deadlines across several courses. In the table, none of these tasks looks extreme by itself. But when the tasks are grouped by week, Week 3 becomes a heavy planning signal.

Now I point to the heatmap. This is the first value of VIP: the student can see the load early, not after the week has already become stressful.

Next, I open the Academic Load Score. The score is broken down into the four rules: assessment load, deadline clustering, expected effort, and task clarity risk. This matters because students should not see a black box. They should see the reason for the signal.

Now I show the Action Card. VIP does not stop at warning the student. It gives a next-step plan: block study time, start the highest-effort task, check unclear tasks, and attend office hour or PASS if stuck.

Next, I run Case 2. The dashboard opens with a Project Milestone prefilled in the Add task form. When I click Add task, the heatmap changes immediately. The Academic Load Score increases and Week 3 becomes Red. The Action Card changes to Red Week guidance: choose the highest-risk task, block two focused work sessions, ask one clarification question within 24 hours, and use the SOS Navigator if uncertainty continues.

This is the core product experience: data becomes visibility, visibility becomes a decision, and the decision becomes action.

## 6:00-7:00 SOS Navigator

Now I show the Academic SOS Navigator through Case 3.

The student marks the Writing Reflection Essay as Unclear. The score increases because unclear work creates planning risk. The Action Card recommends asking one clarification question within 24 hours.

Then the Navigator routes the situation. If the assignment is unclear, the right route is a TA or instructor. If there are too many deadlines, the route is a VIP Ambassador or Peer Advisor. If the student does not know how to start, office hour or PASS may be the right route. If the student is behind across courses, the route is an Academic Advisor.

If academic stress affects daily functioning, VIP routes the student to official VinUni wellbeing support. VIP does not diagnose, provide counseling, or make clinical claims.

## 7:00-8:00 Pilot Plan

The proposed pilot is eight weeks with 50 to 80 opt-in first-year students.

The pilot would include 4 to 6 VIP Ambassadors. Their role is bounded: they help students set up the planner, understand Green, Yellow, and Red weeks, use action cards, and choose support routes. They do not view grades, provide counseling, replace instructors, or monitor students.

Weeks 0 to 1 focus on recruitment, setup, and baseline survey. Weeks 2 to 3 test real academic use. Weeks 4 to 6 collect feedback and refine the template. Weeks 7 to 8 evaluate outcomes and prepare an impact report.

## 8:00-9:00 Impact Metrics and Privacy

VIP measures outcomes it can plausibly affect in eight weeks. These include workload visibility, support route clarity, academic control, action readiness, deadline anticipation, weekly engagement, action card use, early help-seeking, and clarification behavior.

The pilot does not use GPA as a success metric. It does not collect grades, counseling records, clinical data, or private Canvas analytics.

Participation is opt-in. The planner is student-owned. Individual dashboard data is not shared with faculty, advisors, or ambassadors unless the student explicitly asks for help. Pilot reporting uses aggregated and anonymized data.

## 9:00-10:00 Closing Ask

My ask is practical and low-risk.

First, permission to pilot VIP with opt-in first-year volunteers. Second, help connecting the pilot with Peer Advisors, SAM, and relevant support teams. Third, support recruiting students for an eight-week test.

The long-term goal is not to create another platform students must manage. The goal is to build a practical academic wellbeing habit. Every first-year student should be able to say: I know what is coming, I know what to do next, and I know who can help.

Thank you.
