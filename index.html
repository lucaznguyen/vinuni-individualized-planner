About the project

Make hidden workload visible before it becomes hard to manage.

First-year students often receive deadlines course by course. VIP brings the total week into one place, then connects heavy weeks to practical actions and existing support routes.

VIP is A privacy-first academic planning and support-navigation tool.
VIP is not A diagnostic, surveillance, grading, or counseling-replacement system.
Main value Earlier visibility, clearer planning, and better support routing.
🔐

Student-owned

In this demo, data stays in the browser through localStorage. The downloadable sheet is designed for students to own and edit themselves.

🚫

No sensitive academic records

VIP does not ask for GPA, grades, clinical data, counseling records, or private Canvas analytics.

🤝

Support navigation

The tool points students toward the right support route instead of replacing instructors, advisors, PASS, or wellbeing services.

    Methodology

    Transparent Academic Load Score logic.

    The dashboard shows the result first. This section explains the rule-based method behind Green, Yellow, and Red weeks.

    01

    Assessment load

    Small task = +1, medium task = +2, large task = +3. Completed tasks are removed from active workload.

    02

    Deadline clustering

    Two deadlines within 48 hours add +2. Three or more deadlines within 72 hours add +3.

    03

    Expected effort

    A week with 7–11 expected hours adds +1. A week with 12 or more expected hours adds +2.

    04

    Task clarity risk

    Each unclear task adds +1, capped at +2 per week, so uncertainty stays visible but not exaggerated.

    Formula

    Academic Load Score = Assessment Load + Deadline Clustering + Expected Effort + Task Clarity Risk

    Green = 0–4, Yellow = 5–8, Red = 9+. The score is a planning signal, not a diagnosis, ranking, or prediction.

    0–4Green · Maintain routine
    5–8Yellow · Plan early
    9+Red · Act within 24–48 hours
